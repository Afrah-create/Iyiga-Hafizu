About

I am Alex Rivera, a fine arts student studying the dialogue between traditional craft and contemporary observation. My practice moves between the pressure of the press, the precision of the lens, and the immediacy of graphite and charcoal.

Printmaking anchors my process: relief, intaglio, and monotype teach me patience and the beauty of happy accidents. Each pull from the press feels like a collaboration between intention and the material.

Photography & editing extend that curiosity into light and narrative. I chase texture in cities and quiet moments in nature, then shape tone and rhythm in the darkroom or digitally — always in service of emotion, not effect.

Drawing is my daily language — quick studies, long poses, and pages filled with Kampala streets, studio corners, and imagined spaces. It keeps my eye honest and my hand fearless.
