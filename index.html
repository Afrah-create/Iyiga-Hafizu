About

I am Iyiga Hafizu — a Bachelor of Fine Arts student at Makerere University. My practice sits where observation becomes intention: the camera for light and texture, and drawing for form, rhythm, and patience.

Photography & editing is my way of listening. I look for mood in street reflections, skin tones in late afternoon, and the quiet geometry of everyday scenes. Editing helps me keep the emotion while refining the story.

Drawing keeps me grounded. Charcoal and graphite help me measure space, correct perspective gently, and build images from gesture to detail — one careful line at a time.

I’m interested in how Kampala shapes visual memory — the colors we remember, the shadows we carry, and the moments that repeat differently each time.
